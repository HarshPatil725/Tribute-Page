Dr. Ratan Tata

Man who redefined leadership with purpose, compassion, and innovation.

Timeline of Dr. Ratan Tata :

  • 1937: Ratan Naval Tata was born on December 28, 1937, in Mumbai, India, into a prominent Parsi family.
  • 1955: He completed his schooling at the Campion School in Mumbai and later from Cathedral and John Connon School, also in Mumbai.
  • 1975: Completed the Advanced Management Program from Harvard Business School.
  • 1981: Named Chairman of Tata Industries, a holding company for many Tata Group ventures.
  • 1998: Under his leadership, Tata Motors launched the Tata Indica, the first passenger car developed entirely in India.
  • 2000: Awarded the Padma Bhushan, one of India's highest civilian honors.
  • 2001: Set up the Tata Trusts, a philanthropic arm of the group, which continues to support social causes in India.
  • 2008: Honored with the Padma Vibhushan, the second-highest civilian award in India.
  • 2012: Ratan Tata retired as Chairman of Tata Sons, appointing Cyrus Mistry as his successor. However, he continued to be involved in the group as Chairman of Tata Trusts.
  • 2017: Tata Trusts increased its focus on philanthropy, healthcare, and social initiatives under his guidance.
  • 2021: Honored with a special recognition award at the Legatum Center for Development and Entrepreneurship at MIT.
  • 2024: Dies at age of 87.
